Introduction and Statistics

Youth vaping in Canada is rising at an alarming pace. Health Canada survey shows 34% of Canadian students in grades 7 to 12 had tried a vaping product while 20% reported using them within the last 30 days. Another study by University of Waterloo has found that youth vaping in Canada has increased radically by 74% from 8.4% to 14.6%, in-between 2017 and 2018. This shocking data has been observed in a time where the prior surveys up to 2017 had shown a continuous decline in youth smoking.The break-through progress we have made through these years in reducing youth smoking habits had come to an abrupt stop raising worries about new generation of nicotine addiction among these adolescents in the form of vaping. At this point, this new trend of vaping using tobacco marijuana and CBD is skyrocketing among the youth at an shocking rate.

Ways Youth Get Addicted

Vaping is an act of inhaling the aerosol produced from nicotine or other chemical solution vaporized by a battery operated e-cigarettes simulating an genuine smoking experience without the real smoke. They also come in different flavors, which make it more appealing to young people. In addition, these e-cigarettes and chemical solutions comes in different shapes mimicking a sleek pen or flash memory drive or a flashlight, making it harder for parents to identify their children’s addiction into this habit. They are increasingly popular among teenagers because they don’t smell like regular cigarettes and they are easy to hide. Their developing brains make it easier for them to get hooked. Vaping causes serious health risks on these teens by harming the brain development and damaging the lungs. Nicotine is a highly addictive substance which can hamper the brain development and affect their learning, memory, concentration, self-control and increase the risks of other types of addictions. Moreover the toxins and the tiny particles which they breathe-in are harmful including ones that cause cancer. Most of the studies and investigations on the ill-effects of vaping and its agents are still under early stages. Many unknown facts about its dangers including the details about the chemicals present in its vapor content and their long term health impact are not fully unfolded.

Studies show that top reasons for vaping addictions among youth are due to their association with friends and social influences or the flavors of the chemicals or because of the ill-informed facts on the effects of smoking.

Roles of Responsibilities for Everyone

There are serious concerns among Canadian parents, educators, youth and government about the increase of youth vaping. This dangerous speeding of youth vaping addiction has to be put to an end abruptly by helping, preventing and reducing young children in using these products. Every individual has the responsibility in curtailing this habit to spread more among young children. However it is up to the specific youth to educate themselves about the dangers about vaping and ways to stay away from this precarious addiction. Students have to stand up for them to say ‘no’ to any peer pressure or family factors when the topic comes up socially to them.

How Parents can Help

Parents have to lead by setting a positive example by being tobacco-free. They have to talk to their children about its risk and displaying their strong expectations about their children that they remain tobacco-free. These caregivers have to learn about the various forms of these products availability by educating themselves the different shapes and types. They can support their children to look into local programs and websites that help quitting vaping and encourage them to open any dialogue avoiding criticism.


How Schools can Help

School boards has to develop, implement and enforce strict policies on vaping in and around their campuses and at the same time special education and awareness has to be conducted in spreading the truth about this dangerous habit and ways of healthy living by avoiding trapping into this obsession. Students have to be encouraged to discuss any questions about this topic during health classes or during the assembly hours. Placing pamphlets detailing various resources and helplines access to quit the habit and listing the harmful effects of vaping have to be readily available in and around the school campuses stimulating young children to know the bad characters of vaping and quitting the practice.

How Communities can Help

In addition, communities have its own obligation and accountability in this holistic journey of reducing vaping addiction. They have to lead as an example by not accepting any sponsors from vaping product agencies limiting the children’s’ appealing on any advertisements by these companies.

Government Actions

Though current measures in practice are not helping preventing youth vaping, Canadian government agencies are conducting numerous surveys and investigations in reducing this addiction. Extensive studies are under progress to realizing the possibilities of reducing number of flavours in its chemical content, minimizing the nicotine concentration content, regulating the design features of these e-cigarettes,restricting online retail access, limiting packing, or increasing regulatory transparency and openness to hamper the raise in youth vaping. There are various vaping prevention public health campaign, efforts in collaboration with provinces and various other agencies, regulating the advertisement or promotion of these products are also actively in progress. Still, government has to implement strict regulations as quickly as possible to contain this vaping epidemic abruptly to save these young children from this generational curse.

Conclusion

Vaping has made significant impact on Canadian youth in daring means. The rate in which it is spreading among them is dramatically treacherous. Though various agencies are taking progressive actions among restricting this addiction, still much planned coordinated actions and strategies have to be set in place rapidly. Schools and communities have to take every effort to teach youth about the dangers of vaping and encourage them to refrain from initiating this habit or stopping it. Health care organizations have to step up their researches to come up with an effective way to dampen this addiction and finding the facts about the long term effects and hidden dangers to educate among these adolescents. Considering its long term health and economic hazards, the government has to quickly implement various measures and regulations in containing this treat. All stakeholders’ involvement and responsibility are vital to eradicate this epidemic among these young children.
